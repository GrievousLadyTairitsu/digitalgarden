# Plant Tissue
- 4 types of plant tissue: 
	- Meristematic Tissue
	- Dermal Tissue
	- Vascular Tissue
	- Ground Tissue
### Meristematic Tissue
- Similar to human stem cells
- Can undergo rapid mitosis $\rightarrow$ they are undifferentiated
- Develop into specialized cells when they mature
#### 3 types of Meristematic tissue
###### Apical Meristem
- Shoots and roots elongate due to apical (tips) meristem
###### Intercalary Meristem
- Allow grass plants to continue to regrow from the stem
###### Lateral Meristem
- Allows plants to grow in diameter
- Vascular Cambium adds to plant diameter (found in all plants)
- Cork Cambium adds a woody later (only in woody plants)
### Dermal Tissue
- **Dermal Tissue** is the outer protective layer of cells
- The outer most layer of cells (in non-woody plants) is known as the Epidermis
#### 3 Types of Dermal Tissue
###### Guard Cells
- These are a pair of kidney shaped cells that form a structure around the stoma (stomata) where plants have holes for gas exchange
- When **turgid**, they stiffen, as the stoma opens
- Becomes **flaccid** when stoma closes
- Signals within cells control where a stoma is opened or closed
###### Trichomes
- Small hair growths on a plants surface
- Keeps plant surface cool and moist
- Secretes protective substances
- Physically punctures predators
###### Root Hairs
- Tiny structures to increase surface area
	- This helps secure the plant in the soil
	- Maximize nutrient exchange
### Vascular Tissue
- Runs along the length of a plant to deliver water and nutrients
#### Two types of Vascular Tissue
###### Xylem
- Two Tubes:
	- Tracheid (Smaller Diameter)
	- Vessel Elements (Larger-Diameter)
- The xylem is made of hollowed dead cells which transport water and minerals.
###### Phloem
- Phloem are made of two types of cells
	- Sieve tube elements (non-nuclei containing)
	- Companion cells (nuclei-containing)
- Living tissue that transport nutrient (sucrose) where companion cells maintain both cell types
### Ground Tissue
- Ground Tissue accounts for most of the plant
	- Functions include:
	- Photosynthesis
	- Nutrient Storage
	- Structural Support
