#### Lower Respiratory Tract
![Screenshot 2026-04-22 at 3.08.20 PM.png](/img/user/Screenshot%202026-04-22%20at%203.08.20%20PM.png)
###### Explanation
- Three lobes of Right Lung
	- Superior (Top)
	- Middle (Middle)
	- Inferior (Lower)
- Same with the 2 lobes of the left lung 
	- 2 lobes exist because of the heart being left sided
- Trachea
	- The windpipe, where oxygen and carbon dioxide flow through towards the upper respiratory tract
- Epiglottic Cartilage
	- Covers the epiglottis in cartilage
- Tracheal Cartilage
	- Covers the trachea in cartilage
#### Bronchial Tree
![Screenshot 2026-04-22 at 3.10.52 PM.png](/img/user/Screenshot%202026-04-22%20at%203.10.52%20PM.png)
###### Explanation
- Pulmonary Artery Branch
	- Carries deoxygenated blood from the heart
- Pulmonary Vein Branch
	- Carries oxygenated blood to the heart
- Alveolar Ducts and Saccules
	- Single-celled width clusters of Alveolar cells. 
	- Are covered in Capillaries.
	- They secrete [[EXAM REVIEWS/SUBJECT/Bio 11/3.6 Respiratory System#Alveolar cells \|Surfactant]]
- Capillary Network
	- A network of capillaries that conduct gas exchange, to ensure that deoxygenated blood becomes oxygenated before they return to the heart.
- Elastic Fibers
	- Help[[EXAM REVIEWS/SUBJECT/Bio 11/3.6 Respiratory System#Alveolar cells \| Alveoli]] stretch in inhalation and move back during exhalation which helps facilitate gas exchange.
