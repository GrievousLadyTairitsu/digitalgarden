# Plant Categories
#### Taxonomy
###### 3 types of plants
- Non-Vascular (Spores)
- Seedless Vascular (Spores + Vasc. System)
- Seed-generating Vascular Plants (Seeds + Vasc. System)
###### Seed-generating Vascular Characteristics
- Generally more resilient than spores, due to their seed characteristics
Two types:
- **Angiosperms**
- **Gymnosperms**
#### Vascular Plants
Defined to Have
###### Shoot System
- Above ground
- Responsible for making leaves and stems
###### Root System
- Below ground
- Anchors plant in soil
#### Comparing Angio and Gymnosperms
##### Summarily Differences
###### Angiosperms (90% of all plants)
- Reproduce through **flowers**
	- Seeds are contained in the fruit
- Have two Categories
	- Monocot $\rightarrow$ Single Cotyledon (Baby shoot)
	- Dicot $\rightarrow$ Dual Cotyledon (Baby Leaves pair)
- Cotyledon arises upon germination
###### Gymnosperms
- Naked seeds
- Are all conifers (cone-bearing)
- Most are evergreen, and can live in various climate environments (including tropical environments)
