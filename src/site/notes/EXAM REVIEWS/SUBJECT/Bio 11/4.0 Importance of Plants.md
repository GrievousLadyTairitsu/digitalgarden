# Importance of Plants
#### Human Resources
###### Food Source
- 1 in 2 **Canadian** jobs are agricultural related
- Indigenous Communities grow the "three sisters"
	- Corn creates a stalk for the beans, while beans get nitrate in, and squash maintains moisture.
		- Symbiotic Plant-to-Plant relationship (Companion Planting)
- This is due to the **Nitrogen Cycle**
	- Nitrogen gas gets turned into ammonia by nitrogen fixing bacteria in the soil that is then used for plants
###### Commodity
- Plants are used to make materials such as rope, or linen clothing
###### Biochemicals
- Plants generate a large variety of molecules, many of which serve as a natural pesticide.
- Some natural chemicals are useful for human applications
	- Ie. Nunavik Inuit boil Labrador tea flower leaves mixed with oil as a cut ointment. (because of Germacrone in it)
	- They wear bone glasses, and prevent blindness though Limonene squeezed from orange peels
###### Fuel
- Biofuels are a renewable alternative to fossil fuels
	- Carbohydrates produced in plants may be converted into alcohol 
		- Ie. Ethanol (common fuel additive)
###### Erosion Control
- Soil keeps plants in place and plants keep soil in place, meaning that without plants erosion would be a lot worse
###### Tourism
- Important source of revenue and entertainment
